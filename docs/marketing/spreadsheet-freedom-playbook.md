# The Complete Spreadsheet Freedom Playbook

**A practical, plain-English guide for SMB owners who are ready to stop
firefighting data and start making decisions with confidence.**

No fluff, no vendor pitch disguised as a guide. Work through the four parts in
order — most owners finish the whole playbook in under an hour, and the 30-day
plan in exactly that: 30 days.

---

## Part 1 — Why Double-Entry Is Silently Killing Your Growth

Every time you copy a number from an invoice into a spreadsheet, you are not
just losing a minute. You are creating a crack in your data foundation — and
cracks compound.

### The real cost of manual re-entry

- **Time**: a typical 5–50 person business re-keys the same data 3–5 times
  (quote → invoice → stock sheet → cash sheet → month-end report). At 5+ hours
  per week that is 250+ hours per year — six full working weeks.
- **Errors**: industry studies consistently find ~1–4% of manually entered
  cells contain an error. One wrong stock count or price cell propagates into
  every report built on it.
- **Trust**: once the team catches one wrong number, every number is doubted.
  Decisions slow down or get made on gut feel instead.
- **Key-person risk**: the "spreadsheet wizard" becomes a single point of
  failure. When they are on holiday, month-end stalls.

### When "good enough" becomes dangerous

Spreadsheets are fine when one person sells one product line. They become a
business risk when any of these are true:

- Two or more people edit the same file (version chaos, silent overwrites).
- You cannot answer "what is in stock right now?" without calling someone.
- Month-end takes more than one day of reconciliation.
- Your cash position is a guess between bank-statement checks.

**Self-check:** if two or more bullets above describe you, you have passed the
tipping point. Part 2 of this playbook is your way out.

| 7 | Customer, order and invoice data live in separate files/tools | |
| 8 | We delay decisions because we don't trust the numbers | |

### Your score

- **0–6 — Watchful.** Spreadsheets still serve you. Bookmark this playbook and
  re-score every quarter.
- **7–13 — Leaking.** You are paying a hidden data tax every week. Start the
  30-day plan (Part 3) within this quarter.
- **14–24 — Bleeding.** Double-entry is actively constraining growth. Start
  the 30-day plan now — the plan is designed to run *without* disrupting daily
  operations.

### Map where your data lives

Before any migration, list every place business data is kept. For each one
write down: what it holds, who updates it, and where its numbers get re-typed.
Typical list: quoting sheet, invoice tool, stock sheet, bank export, CRM-ish
contact list, month-end master sheet. This map *is* your migration scope —
most businesses find 5–9 sources and 10–20 re-entry touchpoints.

## Part 3 — The 30-Day Migration Plan (Without Disrupting Operations)

The single biggest migration mistake is "big bang": switching everything at
once. The low-risk path is three phases run in parallel with normal business —
the spreadsheets stay alive until the system has proven itself.

### Week 1 — Assess & Map

- [ ] Complete the self-assessment (Part 2) and the data-source map.
- [ ] Mark the **Core Three** flows: inventory, sales, cash. Everything else
      waits.
- [ ] Pick your system (use the vendor questions in Part 4).
- [ ] Create your company in the new system; invite yourself only.
- [ ] Load master data: products, customers, suppliers. Export from your
      spreadsheets — do not re-type.

### Week 2 — Connect the Core Three

- [ ] Enter opening stock counts and current price lists.
- [ ] From Monday: create every **new** quote, order and invoice in the system
      (historical data stays in the old sheets — do not migrate history).
- [ ] Record payments against invoices as they arrive; your cash position is
      now live.
- [ ] Run the old spreadsheet *in parallel* as a safety net. Compare totals on
      Friday. Investigate every mismatch — it is almost always the sheet.

### Week 3 — Eliminate the Chaos

- [ ] Invite the rest of the team; one 30-minute walkthrough each.
- [ ] Turn on automated reports: stock below reorder level, open invoices,
      weekly sales, cash summary.
- [ ] Do the first system-generated month-end (or week-end) close. Time it —
      then compare with your old process.
- [ ] Retire the parallel spreadsheets one by one. Rule: a sheet dies when the
      system has matched it two weeks in a row.

### Week 4 — Scale With Confidence

- [ ] Freeze the last spreadsheet as read-only archive.
- [ ] Reinvest the reclaimed 5+ hours: pipeline follow-up, supplier
      renegotiation, or simply going home on time.
- [ ] Extend beyond the Core Three only now: accounting integration, website
      orders, warehouse locations — one module per month, never more.
- [ ] Book a 15-minute monthly "numbers review" — with data you finally trust.

**Rule of thumb:** if any week slips, let it slip. Never skip the parallel-run
safety net to catch up.

## Part 4 — Questions to Ask Any ERP Vendor Before You Sign

Ask these verbatim. Vague answers are answers.

**Cost & lock-in**
1. What is the *total* first-year cost for my team size — licences,
   implementation, training, support?
2. What does it cost to leave? Can I export **all** my data (not a PDF dump)
   in an open format at any time?
3. Is the software open source? If you disappear, can someone else run it?

**Fit & scope**
4. Can I start with only inventory, sales and cash — and add modules later?
5. What does a 5–50 person company implementation actually look like? Talk me
   through the last one you did.
6. Does it run on the devices we use (web, phone, tablet, desktop)?

**Operations**
7. How do updates happen, and have they ever broken a customer's workflow?
8. Where is my data hosted, who can see it, and how is it backed up?
9. What happens to my price when I grow from 5 users to 25?

**Red flags:** multi-year contracts before a pilot, per-module pricing that
doubles the quote, "we'll need to scope that" for question 1, and any vendor
who cannot demo *your* workflow live.

## Part 5 — Calculate Your ROI Before You Commit

Use last month's real numbers. Conservative estimates only.

### Step 1 — What spreadsheets cost you today (per month)

| Item | How to estimate | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| Re-entry time | hours/week × hourly cost × 4.3 | 5 h × €35 × 4.3 = **€752** |
| Error correction | incidents × hours to fix × hourly cost | 3 × 2 h × €35 = **€210** |
| Stock misses | stock-outs or overstock write-offs / month | **€300** |
| Month-end overrun | extra days × day cost | 1 × €280 = **€280** |
| **Total hidden cost** | | **€1,542 / month** |

### Step 2 — What the system costs (per month)

Licence/hosting + (one-time setup ÷ 24 months). For an open-source system in
this example: €150 + €50 = **€200 / month**.

### Step 3 — The verdict

```
ROI = (hidden cost recovered − system cost) ÷ system cost
    = (1,542 − 200) ÷ 200  ≈  6.7×  — payback in the first month
```

If your own numbers show payback longer than 6 months, stay on spreadsheets
and re-score quarterly. Most businesses past the Part 2 tipping point see
payback in 4–8 weeks.
